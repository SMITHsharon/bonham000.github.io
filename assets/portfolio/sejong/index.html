King Sejong the Great

세종대왕, 世宗大王

Fourth Ruler of the Joseon Dynasty of Korea

A Tribute:

"If the people prosper, how can the king not prosper with them? And if the people do not prosper, how can the king prosper without them?"

    A Timeline:

  • 1397: Sejong is born as the 3rd son in the 4th generation of the Joseon Dynasty to Queen Wonkyong and King Taejong.
  • 1409: Sejong is officially named a prince.
  • 1413: Prince Sejong assumes the title of Grand Prince.
  • 1418: Prince Sejong is inaugurated as Crown Prince in place of his older brother, Yangnyong.
  • 1418: Prince Sejong assumes the throne as the 4th king of the Joseon dynasty.
  • 1420: Emperor Sejong founds an advanced school.
  • 1441: Emperor Sejong invents the pluviometer (rain gauge).
  • 1443: Emperor Sejong successfully finishes the creation of hangul (the great writing), the new Korean language.
  • 1446: Joseon Korea starts using hangul, now the official language of Joseon Korea.
  • 1450: Sejong the Great dies and his son, Prince Munjong, succeeds him as the 5th king of the Joseon Dynasty.

Conclusion

King Sejong the Great was the fourth ruler of the Joseon Dynasty of Korea, and a beloved Korean folk hero. Highly educated and a gifted military strategist, he is most famous for commissioning the creation of the phonetic Korean alphabet Hangul by scholars of the Hall of Worthies, despite strong opposition from officials educated in Hanja; the "Hangul" alphabet continues to be used today. King Sejong also established a library and an institution for scholarly research, and promoted men of talent to positions of authority in his government. Under his patronage Korean literature and culture flourished. He encouraged technological advances in weaponry and initiated the use of printing presses
